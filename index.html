Why do we need Renewable Energy ?
Energy consumption in GCC countries has grown at an average of 6% per year since 2000 , outpacing GDP growth ( 5%) and population growth ( 4%). With rising domestic demand, GCC countries are moving towards diversifying the energy mix towards alternative sources, including renewables.
Renewable Energy Sources in GCC Countries
Source: IRENA (2016), ‘Renewable Energy Market Analysis: The GCC Region’. IRENA, Abu Dhabi.
Wind
more than 56% of the GCC’s surface area has significant potential for wind deployment. Covering just 1% of this area could translate into an equivalent 60 GW of capacity.
Solar
more than 59% of the GCC’s surface area has significant potential for solar PV deployment. Developing just 1% of this area can potentially result in 470 GW of solar PV capacity.
Biomass
Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Bahrain and Kuwait are counted as world’s largest waste producers in terms of per capita waste generation which is more than 2kg per day in some countries.
